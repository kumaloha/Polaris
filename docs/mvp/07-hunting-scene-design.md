# Hunting MVP Scene Design

## 1. Reduction Principle

The previous design is useful as long-term direction but too complex for the first playable.

The MVP keeps one short loop:

> Invest in self to enter the room, use the first eye to read men, play party boundary decisions, continue through a short after-party beat, then receive portfolio feedback.

Target:

- 3 minutes to understand.
- 10 minutes to complete the first run.
- 5 screens, 3 player resources, 4 party actions.

Out of MVP:

- Large city map.
- Full weekly life simulator.
- Nine self stats.
- Ten-year prophecy.
- Long relationship routes.
- Free-form chat.
- Large wardrobe.

## 2. Five Scenes

### Scene 1: Get Ready

Merged from apartment, girlfriend circle, and persona studio.

Player chooses:

1. One self-investment card.
2. One girlfriend support.
3. One persona.

Question answered:

> Why can I enter this room tonight?

### Scene 2: First Eye

Pre-party due diligence.

Player reads three men through:

- Basic profile.
- Chat logs.
- One risk or opportunity.

Player selects:

- Primary target.
- Backup target.

Question answered:

> What kind of man is he right now?

No future read here.

### Scene 3: Party Level

The core gameplay scene.

Fixed MVP structure:

- 6 rounds.
- 1 primary target.
- 1 backup target.
- 1 leverage man.
- 1 drain man.
- 1 girlfriend support.

Question answered:

> How do I make the right man approach while exiting the wrong men?

### Scene 4: After-Party Investment Decision

Merged from phone, short dating event, and girlfriend gossip.

Player goes through:

1. Men send messages.
2. Girlfriend gives one sharp comment or clue.
3. Player decides whether to date, and who to date.
4. The selected man enters one short date test.

Question answered:

> Do I enter a relationship beat, and with whom?

### Scene 5: Portfolio Feedback

The second eye.

It is post-investment feedback, not prophecy.

Player sees:

- Self outcome.
- Men outcome.
- Preference evidence.
- Recommended next move.

Question answered:

> Did this investment make my portfolio stronger or weaker?

## 3. Three Player Resources

| Resource | Meaning | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Energy | Action capacity tonight. | Party actions, replies, and date tests consume it. |
| Charm | Baseline attraction and room entry. | Raises approach probability and unlocks rooms. |
| Position | Girlfriend backing, social place, and persona credibility. | Unlocks better parties and makes men treat the player more seriously. |

Other self-investment dimensions, such as exit power, cashflow, judgment, and emotional reserve, appear through cards and feedback copy for now.

## 6. Scene 3 Details: Party Level

Fixed at 6 rounds.

Each round:

1. Room event appears.
2. Men act first.
3. Player chooses one action.
4. Energy, attention, preference, risk, and persona consistency update.

### Four Actions

| Action | Meaning | Main Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engage | Give interest. | Male interest rises, Energy -1. |
| Boundary | Reject low-cost access. | Filters weak men and tests preference. |
| Social Proof | Use attention from others. | Target attention rises through Position check. |
| Exit | End interaction. | Saves Energy and avoids drain. |

Cut from MVP:

- Smile.
- Tease.
- Hold.
- Test.
- Friend Rescue as a normal button.

Maya rescue appears only as a support trigger.

### Active Approach Men

Only two types in MVP:

- Leverage Man: can raise social proof.
- Drain Man: consumes Energy until exited or rescued.

### MVP Preference Evidence

Only four evidence types:

- He turns vague chat into a concrete plan.
- He adjusts after a boundary instead of disappearing.
- He pulls the player away from a low-value interaction.
- He gives the player social position in front of others.

## 7. Scene 4 Details: After-Party Investment Decision

Duration: around 3 minutes.

This is the player's first explicit relationship investment decision:

> Should I date anyone? Who gets real energy? Who stays observed? Who gets cut?

### Step 1: Messages

Examples:

- Adrian: "Saturday 8. I'll book the place."
- Evan: "Still awake? I keep thinking of you."
- Leo: "I kept thinking about what you said."

### Step 2: Girlfriend Comment

One line, not a long review.

Examples:

- Nina on Evan: "He is not deep. He is cheap at night."
- Claire on Adrian: "The venue matters less than whether he keeps time."
- Maya on Leo: "Observe him, but do not fund the fantasy."

### Step 3: Relationship Choice

The player assigns a state to each man.

MVP states:

| State | Meaning | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Date | Put him into the short date test. | Costs Energy and gives stronger feedback. |
| Observe | Do not invest yet; watch his next action. | Saves Energy and keeps option open. |
| Test | Require a concrete action first. | Can become Date if he responds well. |
| Cut | Stop investing. | Saves Energy and removes a drain. |

Rules:

- The player can Date at most one man per run.
- The player can Observe one backup.
- Others should usually be Cut.
- Dating costs Energy, so the player cannot date every man.

### Step 4: Player Response

For the selected man, the player chooses one response:

| Response | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Accept | Continue. |
| Ask Plan | Require time, place, or action. |
| Delay | Do not give immediate access. |
| Cut | Stop investing. |

### Step 5: Date Test

Only the Date man triggers this.

MVP event:

> First Date Time Change

Adrian tries to move the dinner later.

Player options:

- Accept.
- Ask him to rearrange.
- Delay.
- Cut.

This tests:

- Time respect.
- Action quality.
- Boundary.

## 8. Scene 5 Details: Portfolio Feedback

The second eye should feel like a game settlement screen, not a report deck.

### Self Outcome

Show only three items:

| Item | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Energy | -3, good control. |
| Charm / Position | +1, Rare Girl image remembered. |
| Control | +1, refused low-cost midnight access. |

Control can be a settlement metric, not a permanent stat yet.

### Men Outcome

One sentence per man:

- Adrian: concrete action; worth testing.
- Evan: high sugar, low action; exit recommended.
- Leo: low-volume but specific; observe.

### Player Decision

Only four buttons:

- Add.
- Observe.
- Test.
- Exit.

## 10. MVP Acceptance Criteria

The MVP is working if:

- It has exactly five playable scenes.
- The first screen is a game get-ready scene, not a dashboard.
- The player has only three core resources.
- The party has only four player actions.
- First Eye is chat-log due diligence only.
- Second Eye is post-investment portfolio feedback only.
- The player can feel "I saw him correctly" or "I exited correctly" within one 10-minute run.
