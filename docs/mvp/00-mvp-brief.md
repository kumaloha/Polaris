# MVP Brief

## Purpose

Define the first playable MVP for a female-oriented casual relationship strategy game. This document locks the product promise, target player, module priority, platform priority, MVP scope, and non-goals.

## Product Promise

Players come back every day to read men, upgrade themselves, and occasionally collect or regret a high-emotion relationship investment.

The sharp emotional hook:

> You thought you were screening red flags. Then you realized you might be someone else's.

## Target Player

Primary audience:

- Women aged 18-35.
- Mobile-first casual game players.
- Users who consume dating discourse, romance content, idol/celebrity fantasy, short-form relationship advice, personality quizzes, and interactive story content.
- Launch-market priority: United States first, then English-speaking Europe, then Japan localization exploration.

Psychological profile:

- Wants romantic fantasy without surrendering agency.
- Enjoys reading social signals, decoding texts, and discussing relationship behavior.
- Wants to feel desirable, selective, and emotionally intelligent.
- Likes the idea of attractive men, but distrusts low-commitment performance.

## Platform Priority

**iOS first. Android later.**

Rationale:

- iOS is the better first validation surface for a premium-feeling female casual MVP.
- iOS users generally support stronger IAP and subscription testing, which matters for outfits, premium cases, hints, and relationship rewind mechanics.
- The game depends on visual polish, typography, chat UI, and shareable result cards; iOS gives a cleaner first product target.
- Starting with one platform reduces UI QA and monetization complexity while the core question is still unproven.

Android should follow after the MVP proves:

- Players finish cases.
- Players enjoy red-flag identification.
- Players use self-improvement/persona cards.
- Players emotionally respond to relationship investment nodes.

## Core Modules

### 1. Male Signal Detection

Frequency: highest.

This is the home screen, the main loop, and the highest-volume content surface.

Player fantasy:

- I can read the situation.
- I can identify risk before I get attached.
- I can respond with control instead of panic.

### 2. Self Improvement

Frequency: medium.

Self-improvement supports case performance. It should not become the main game in the MVP.

Player fantasy:

- I become more attractive, sharper, and more selective.
- I am not waiting to be chosen; I am improving my market position.

### 3. High-Return Relationship Investment

Frequency: lowest.

This is a periodic high-emotion node, not a high-frequency task. It represents relationship, attention, time, emotional, and identity investment inside the game.

It is not financial investment, PUA instruction, or real-world manipulation advice.

Player fantasy:

- I can decide who deserves more of me.
- I can hold, fold, or double down.
- I may be rewarded, exposed, or punished by my own judgment.

## MVP Loop

1. Open the app to today's case.
2. Choose a lightweight persona/self-improvement card.
3. Read a man's profile, chat, and behavior clues.
4. Tag signals: red flag, green flag, potential, performance, boundary test.
5. Choose a response strategy.
6. Receive a result report.
7. Earn growth points.
8. Periodically make a relationship investment decision.

## MVP Scope

The first MVP should include:

- 10 playable cases.
- 5 male archetypes.
- 4 persona cards.
- 6 core signal categories.
- 4 response strategy families.
- 3 relationship investment nodes.
- Result reports that are short, sharp, and shareable.

## Non-Goals

The MVP is not:

- A full otome game.
- A full dress-up game.
- A long-form dating simulator.
- A male-hating lecture game.
- A therapy app.
- A real-world dating advice certification tool.
- A financial investment metaphor stretched into relationship manipulation.

## Success Definition

The MVP succeeds if a small test audience can:

- Explain the game in one sentence.
- Finish multiple cases without feeling lectured.
- Enjoy the result report enough to screenshot or share.
- Understand that self-improvement supports detection.
- Remember at least one relationship investment outcome.
