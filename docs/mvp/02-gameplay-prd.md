# Gameplay PRD

## Purpose

Define the MVP gameplay loop, module frequencies, case structure, scoring, progression, and the golden path.

## MVP Product Shape

Vertical mobile casual game for iOS-first testing.

The MVP is built around one high-frequency loop:

> Read a male signal, judge what it means, choose how to respond, receive a sharp result.

Self-improvement and relationship investment support that loop. They do not replace it.

## Module Frequency

| Module | Frequency | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Male Signal Detection | 70% | Main gameplay, home screen, daily return reason. |
| Self Improvement | 20% | Loadout, progression, unlock support. |
| Relationship Investment | 10% | Periodic emotional peak, long-tail consequence. |

## Core Loop

1. Case appears on the home screen.
2. Player selects one persona card.
3. Player reads profile, chat, and behavior clues.
4. Player tags signals.
5. Player chooses a response strategy.
6. Game reveals result report.
7. Player receives growth rewards.
8. Every several cases, player makes a relationship investment decision.

## Case Anatomy

Each case contains:

- Hook title.
- Male archetype.
- Social context.
- 5-8 chat or behavior clues.
- 2-4 signal tags to identify.
- 1 response decision.
- Result report.
- Growth rewards.
- Optional relationship carryover.

## Persona Cards

Persona cards are light strategic loadouts selected before a case.

### Sweet Girl

- Effect: intimacy +1, pressure -1.
- Risk: may attract protective or controlling behavior.

### Ice Queen

- Effect: boundary +2, initial warmth -1.
- Risk: may suppress vulnerable clues.

### Career Muse

- Effect: high-value attention +1, seriousness +1.
- Risk: may trigger status competition.

### Party Star

- Effect: tension +2, social reach +1.
- Risk: short-term desire noise +1.

## Signal Categories

MVP signal categories:

- Sincerity.
- Avoidant commitment.
- Boundary test.
- Emotional manipulation.
- Short-term desire.
- Growth potential.

Each signal should be readable from behavior, not from narrator explanation alone.

## Response Strategy Families

### Warm Probe

The player asks a clear but non-aggressive follow-up.

Use when:

- The signal is ambiguous.
- The player wants more data.

### Boundary Set

The player states a limit and watches the response.

Use when:

- The man is testing access, speed, or emotional debt.

### Cool Down

The player slows the interaction without dramatic confrontation.

Use when:

- The player suspects performance or low commitment.

### Mirror Test

The player reflects the behavior back or requests consistency.

Use when:

- The man is using charm, vagueness, or future talk.

## Scoring Model

### Player Stats

- Charm: unlocks higher-attention cases and social contexts.
- Judgment: reveals extra clues and improves report accuracy.
- Boundary: unlocks stronger response options.
- Social Rank: unlocks higher-status scenes.

### Case Scores

- Accuracy: did the player identify the true signal?
- Control: did the response improve player agency?
- Risk Exposure: did the response reveal hidden risk?
- Emotional Cost: did the player overinvest, escalate, or chase?

### Relationship Scores

- Tension: romantic/sexual electricity.
- Trust: long-term possibility.
- Control: who sets pace and terms.
- Sunk Cost: player investment already committed.

## Outcomes

Cases should not always resolve into right/wrong answers. The MVP supports five outcome families:

- Clean read: player identifies the signal and exits or advances.
- Risk revealed: player response causes hidden risk to surface.
- Respect earned: boundary produces better behavior.
- Ambiguity remains: player gains partial data.
- Reversal: player learns her own strategy created risk.

## Golden Path Example

### Case

Title: **The 1:37 AM Text**

Archetype: Late Night Texter.

Context: He has texted warmly for three nights but has not made a daytime plan.

### Persona Selection

Player selects **Ice Queen**.

Effect:

- Boundary +2.
- Initial warmth -1.

### Chat / Case Reading

He sends:

> I don't know why, but I always think of you at night.

Then:

> You're different. I can't really talk like this with other people.

Then:

> This week is crazy, but stay up with me for a bit?

Behavior clue:

- He viewed the player's story at noon but did not reply until 1:37 AM.

### Signal Tagging

Correct signals:

- Short-term desire.
- Avoidant commitment.
- Emotional timing control.

Plausible wrong signal:

- Deep vulnerability.

### Response Strategy

Player selects **Boundary Set**:

> I like talking to you, but I'm not doing midnight-only intimacy. If you want to know me, make a real plan.

### Result Report

Report:

- True type: high-tension, low-commitment texter.
- Risk: 74.
- Control change: player +14.
- Emotional cost: low.
- Insight: he wanted access without structure.

Unlock:

- Boundary +6.
- Judgment +4.
- Response line saved to collection.

### Self-Improvement Reward

Player earns points to upgrade **Boundary** from level 1 to level 2.

New ability:

- See "timing mismatch" clues in future cases.

### Relationship Investment Node

After several similar cases, the same man returns with a daytime invitation.

Player options:

- Add: accept a structured date.
- Hold: keep him as low-priority contact.
- Fold: end the thread.
- Test: accept only if he plans time/place.

This is relationship investment: time, attention, emotional access, and identity exposure. It is not financial investment or real-world manipulation instruction.

## MVP Content Quantity

First playable target:

- 10 cases.
- 5 male archetypes.
- 4 persona cards.
- 6 signal categories.
- 4 response strategies.
- 3 relationship investment nodes.
- 5 result report templates.

## Acceptance Criteria

- A player can complete one case in 2-4 minutes.
- The home screen always points to a case first.
- Persona cards affect interpretation or response options.
- Result reports are short enough to screenshot.
- Investment nodes appear after case clusters, not after every case.
- No required system assumes full romance progression.
