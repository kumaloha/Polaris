# Content Bible

## Purpose

Define the repeatable content grammar for MVP cases: male archetypes, signal labels, response strategies, report tone, and investment nodes.

## Content Rule

Every case must create one clean question:

> What does his behavior actually mean, and what should I do next?

The answer should be emotionally satisfying but not preachy.

## Male Archetypes

### Late Night Texter

Surface:

- Intimate, poetic, emotionally available after midnight.

Hidden risk:

- Wants access without structure.

Useful signals:

- Timing mismatch.
- No daytime plan.
- Fast intimacy.

### Future Talker

Surface:

- Talks about trips, future homes, big plans, destiny.

Hidden risk:

- Uses imagined future to avoid present commitment.

Useful signals:

- No concrete date.
- Promises scale faster than action.
- Makes the player feel chosen before earning trust.

### Soft Nice Guy

Surface:

- Gentle, available, never openly demanding.

Hidden risk:

- Low responsibility hidden behind softness.

Useful signals:

- Avoids decisions.
- Makes the player manage emotional clarity.
- Says "whatever you want" but resents outcomes.

### Alpha Performer

Surface:

- High-status, charming, socially fluent, desired by others.

Hidden risk:

- Performs preference without exclusivity.

Useful signals:

- Too polished.
- Same lines fit many women.
- Public confidence, private vagueness.

### Hidden Potential

Surface:

- Less polished, maybe awkward, not yet high-status.

Hidden risk:

- Could be genuinely growing or simply underdeveloped.

Useful signals:

- Accountability under pressure.
- Consistency without performance.
- Action before language.

## Response Strategies

### Warm Probe

Template:

> I hear you. What would that look like in real life?

Purpose:

- Turns feeling into evidence.

### Boundary Set

Template:

> I like talking to you, but I need this to be clearer than late-night chemistry.

Purpose:

- Tests respect and seriousness.

### Cool Down

Template:

> I'm going to slow this down and see how it feels.

Purpose:

- Removes reward from unclear behavior.

### Mirror Test

Template:

> That sounds good. What are you actually planning?

Purpose:

- Makes charm become action or collapse.

### Direct Exit

Template:

> I don't think this works for me. Take care.

Purpose:

- Preserves agency when evidence is strong.

### Strategic Hold

Template:

> I am not closing the door, but I'm not investing more until I see consistency.

Purpose:

- Turns ambiguity into low-cost observation.

## Result Report Template

Each report should include:

- Your read.
- Actual signal.
- Risk level.
- Control shift.
- Emotional cost.
- One concise insight.
- One unlocked reward or line.

Example:

> Actual signal: Avoidant commitment.  
> Risk: 78.  
> Control: +12.  
> Insight: he offered intensity without structure.

## MVP Case Frameworks

### Case 1: The 1:37 AM Text

Archetype: Late Night Texter.

Main question:

- Is this intimacy or access without structure?

Correct signals:

- Timing mismatch.
- Short-term desire.
- Avoidant commitment.

Golden path:

- Chat/case reading: three late-night messages plus daytime story-view clue.
- Signal tagging: player marks timing mismatch and avoidant commitment.
- Response strategy: Boundary Set.
- Result report: "He wanted access without structure."
- Self-improvement card: Ice Queen increases boundary and reveals timing clue.
- Investment node: later invitation creates Add/Hold/Fold/Test decision.

## Investment Node Templates

### Add

Meaning:

- Increase time, attention, and relational access.

Use when:

- Evidence shows consistency and respect.

### Hold

Meaning:

- Maintain low-cost contact without deeper investment.

Use when:

- Potential exists but evidence is incomplete.

### Fold

Meaning:

- Exit and protect emotional bandwidth.

Use when:

- Risk is high or behavior is inconsistent.

### Test

Meaning:

- Offer a clear condition and observe action.

Use when:

- The player needs one decisive proof point.

## Voice

Reports should be crisp:

- "He offered intensity without structure."
- "This is not vulnerability yet. It is access-seeking."
- "He respected the boundary. That matters more than the line."
- "You kept him warm, but he stopped waiting."

Avoid:

- Long moral essays.
- Clinical therapy language.
- Universal claims about men.
- Advice that sounds like real-world manipulation instruction.
